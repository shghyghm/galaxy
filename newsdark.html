An eclipsing binary millisecond pulsar discovered by FAST

Led a research team Using data from the FAST Radio Telescope, Professor Pan Zichen and Professor Di Li of the National Astronomical Observatory at the Chinese Academy of Sciences (NAOC) discovered a double millisecond pulsar in the M92 star cluster. Also known as the PSR J1717 + 4307A and M92A, it is the first pulsar identified in Messier 92. Its rotation period is 3.16 milliseconds. Follow-up of observations showed that this dual system is in a circular orbit with a rotation period of 0.2 days and a radius of 120,000 km. Its companion star is 0.18 times the mass of the Sun and is becoming a relatively massive star. Due to the compactness of the orbit, the material from the companion star is swallowed by the pulsar. Such a dual system has been called the "red-backed spider". This pulsar, like this type of spider, tends to accumulate material from its companion star.
